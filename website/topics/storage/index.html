Why Secondary Storage?

üîå

RAM is Volatile

RAM loses all data when power is off. We need somewhere to store data permanently.

üì¶

Large Capacity Needed

Modern files (games, videos, photos) need much more space than RAM can provide.

üí∞

Cost Effective

Secondary storage is much cheaper per GB than RAM.

üì§

Portability

Some storage can be removed and transferred between devices.

Types of Secondary Storage

üß≤

Magnetic Storage

How it works:

Data is stored by magnetising tiny areas on a spinning disk or tape. A read/write head moves across the surface to access data.

Examples:

Hard Disk Drive (HDD)

Internal/external drives with spinning platters. 500GB - 20TB typical.

Magnetic Tape

Long strips of magnetic tape. Used for backups and archives.

Characteristics:

CapacityVery High (up to 20TB+)
SpeedMedium (80-200 MB/s)
PortabilityMedium (external HDDs portable)
DurabilityLow (moving parts can break)
ReliabilityMedium (can fail over time)
CostLow cost per GB
‚ö°

Solid State Storage

How it works:

Data is stored in flash memory chips using electrical charges. No moving parts - all electronic.

Examples:

Solid State Drive (SSD)

Fast internal/external drives. 128GB - 8TB typical.

USB Flash Drive

Small portable "memory sticks". 8GB - 1TB typical.

SD Card

Used in cameras, phones, games consoles. 16GB - 1TB typical.

Characteristics:

CapacityMedium-High (up to 8TB)
SpeedVery Fast (500-7000 MB/s)
PortabilityHigh (small, light, no moving parts)
DurabilityHigh (no moving parts)
ReliabilityHigh (resistant to shock)
CostHigher cost per GB
üíø

Optical Storage

How it works:

A laser reads tiny pits and lands on the surface of a spinning disc. Data is encoded in the pattern of reflections.

Examples:

CD (Compact Disc)

700MB capacity. Music, small files.

DVD (Digital Versatile Disc)

4.7GB single layer, 8.5GB dual layer. Films, software.

Blu-ray Disc

25GB single layer, 50GB dual layer. HD films, games.

Characteristics:

CapacityLow (700MB - 50GB)
SpeedSlow (1-50 MB/s)
PortabilityHigh (thin, light discs)
DurabilityMedium (scratches damage data)
ReliabilityMedium (degrade over time)
CostVery low per disc

Choosing the Right Storage

Different situations need different storage solutions:

üéÆ Gaming PC

Needs: Fast loading, large capacity

Best choice: SSD for OS and games, HDD for extra storage

üì∏ Photographer

Needs: Portable, works in camera

Best choice: SD cards for camera, external HDD for archive

üé¨ Film Distribution

Needs: Cheap to mass produce, standard format

Best choice: Blu-ray or DVD

üè¢ Business Backup

Needs: Huge capacity, long-term storage

Best choice: Magnetic tape (cheapest per TB)

üì± Smartphone

Needs: Small, fast, shock-resistant

Best choice: Built-in flash storage (SSD technology)

üìö Student

Needs: Portable, affordable, transfer files

Best choice: USB flash drive

Key Vocabulary

Secondary Storage

Non-volatile storage that keeps data when power is off.

Volatile

Storage that loses data when power is removed (like RAM).

Non-volatile

Storage that retains data without power (secondary storage).

Capacity

How much data the storage can hold (GB, TB).

Access Speed

How quickly data can be read or written (MB/s).

Portability

How easily the storage can be moved between devices.

Durability

How well storage withstands physical damage.

Reliability

How consistently the storage works without failure.

Exam Tips

Common Exam Questions

  • "Explain why secondary storage is needed" (2 marks)
  • "Compare HDD and SSD for [scenario]" (4 marks)
  • "Recommend a storage device for [use case] and justify" (3 marks)
  • "State two advantages and two disadvantages of [storage type]" (4 marks)

Key Points to Remember

  • HDD = cheap, high capacity, but slow and fragile
  • SSD = fast, durable, but more expensive
  • Optical = cheap to distribute, but low capacity
  • Always justify your choice based on the scenario!
  • Consider: capacity, speed, portability, durability, cost
