Predict: Storage Scenarios

Think about which storage type would be best for each situation.

How to use this activity:

  1. Read each scenario carefully
  2. Think about which storage type would be best and why
  3. Click "Reveal Answer" to check your prediction
  4. Consider the characteristics: capacity, speed, portability, durability, cost

Scenario 1: Gaming Laptop

A gamer is buying a new laptop. They want games to load as fast as possible and need to store about 500GB of games. They'll be carrying the laptop to friends' houses regularly.

Which storage type would be best? Why?

Answer: Solid State Drive (SSD)

Why SSD is best:

  • Speed: SSDs are 5-50x faster than HDDs - games load in seconds
  • Durability: No moving parts means safe to transport
  • Portability: Light and shock-resistant for carrying around

Why not HDD? Moving parts could be damaged when transported, and loading would be much slower.

Why not Optical? Way too slow for gaming and limited capacity.

Key factors: Speed + Durability for portable gaming

Scenario 2: Music Distribution

A record label wants to sell physical copies of an album. They need to produce 10,000 copies as cheaply as possible. Each album is about 600MB of audio files.

Which storage type would be best for distribution?

Answer: CD (Optical Storage)

Why CD is best:

  • Cost: CDs cost pennies each to mass-produce
  • Capacity: 700MB is perfect for an album
  • Portability: Thin, light, easy to ship
  • Standard format: Works in any CD player

Why not USB drives? Cost ¬£2-5 each - far too expensive for 10,000 copies!

Why not HDD? Way too expensive and bulky for music distribution.

Key factors: Low cost for mass production

Scenario 3: Security Camera System

A shop needs to store 30 days of security footage from 8 cameras recording 24/7. This requires about 6TB of storage. The footage is recorded but rarely watched back.

Which storage type would be best?

Answer: Hard Disk Drive (HDD)

Why HDD is best:

  • Capacity: 6TB HDDs are readily available and affordable
  • Cost: Much cheaper per TB than SSDs
  • Speed: Writing video doesn't need SSD speeds
  • Continuous writing: HDDs handle constant writing well

Why not SSD? Too expensive for 6TB and constant writing can wear out flash memory.

Why not Optical? Would need hundreds of discs and constant disc swapping!

Key factors: High capacity + Low cost for bulk storage

Scenario 4: Photography Trip

A wildlife photographer is going on a 2-week trip to photograph animals. They'll be outdoors in all weather, and their camera uses SD cards. They expect to take about 200GB of photos.

Which storage solution would be best?

Answer: Multiple SD Cards (Solid State)

Why SD cards are best:

  • Compatibility: SD cards work directly in cameras
  • Durability: No moving parts, waterproof options available
  • Portability: Tiny and lightweight
  • Reliability: Work in hot, cold, and humid conditions

Good backup strategy: Bring a portable SSD to copy photos to at night.

Why not HDD? Fragile moving parts could break with outdoor use.

Key factors: Durability + Portability for outdoor use

Scenario 5: Bank Data Archive

A bank needs to archive 10 years of transaction records (50TB of data) for legal compliance. The data is rarely accessed - maybe once a year for audits. Cost is a major concern.

Which storage type would be most appropriate?

Answer: Magnetic Tape

Why magnetic tape is best:

  • Cost: Cheapest per TB for large archives
  • Capacity: Single tapes hold 6-15TB each
  • Longevity: Tapes last 30+ years when stored properly
  • Access pattern: Perfect for "write once, read rarely" data

Why not SSD? Way too expensive for 50TB of rarely-accessed data.

Why not HDD? More expensive and HDDs degrade if left unused.

Key factors: Lowest cost for long-term archival

Scenario 6: Student Homework

A student needs to save their homework and transfer it between home and school computers. Files are mostly Word documents and PDFs totalling about 5GB.

Which storage device would be most suitable?

Answer: USB Flash Drive

Why USB flash drive is best:

  • Portability: Fits on a keychain, easy to carry
  • Compatibility: Works in any computer's USB port
  • Capacity: 8-32GB is plenty for documents
  • Cost: Affordable (¬£5-15)
  • Durability: No moving parts, survives being in a bag

Alternative: Cloud storage is even better if internet is available!

Key factors: Portability + Ease of use for small files

Scenario 7: Film Studio Editing

A film editor needs to work with 4K video files. They need to scrub through footage smoothly, access files instantly, and work with about 2TB of raw video. Speed is critical.

Which storage type would be best?

Answer: NVMe SSD

Why NVMe SSD is best:

  • Speed: 3000-7000 MB/s - essential for 4K editing
  • Random access: Instant scrubbing through footage
  • Capacity: 2TB NVMe SSDs are available
  • Reliability: Consistent performance under heavy use

Why not HDD? Too slow - would cause stuttering when scrubbing.

Cost consideration: Expensive but necessary for professional work.

Key factors: Maximum speed for professional video work

Scenario 8: Software Distribution (Old School)

A company in a region with limited internet wants to distribute their 20GB software to customers physically. They'll produce about 500 copies.

Which optical format would be most appropriate?

Answer: Blu-ray Disc

Why Blu-ray is best:

  • Capacity: 25GB single layer fits the 20GB software
  • Cost: Cheaper than USB drives for 500 copies
  • Standard format: Can be read by Blu-ray drives

Why not DVD? Only 4.7GB - would need 5 discs per customer!

Why not USB? More expensive at 500 units.

Note: Many modern laptops don't have optical drives, so a USB option should also be offered.

Key factors: Capacity vs cost for physical distribution

Scenario 9: Dropping a Device

A laptop with an HDD is accidentally dropped from a desk while it's running. Another laptop with an SSD is also dropped.

Which laptop is more likely to have data loss? Why?

Answer: The HDD laptop is more likely to have data loss

Why HDD is more vulnerable:

  • Moving parts: HDD has spinning platters and a moving read/write head
  • Head crash: Impact can cause the head to hit the platter
  • Physical damage: Moving parts can be knocked out of alignment

Why SSD is safer:

  • No moving parts: Data stored in flash chips
  • Shock resistant: Can withstand significant impact
Key concept: Moving parts = vulnerability to physical shock

Scenario 10: Budget Desktop Computer

A family needs a budget desktop for homework, web browsing, and storing family photos (about 2TB total). They want to spend as little as possible on storage.

Which storage solution offers the best value?

Answer: Small SSD + Large HDD (Hybrid approach)

Best budget solution:

  • 256GB SSD: For Windows and programs - fast boot and app loading
  • 2TB HDD: For photos and files - cheap bulk storage

Why this combination?

  • Get SSD speed where it matters (OS, programs)
  • Get HDD capacity where speed doesn't matter (photos, documents)
  • Total cost is much lower than a 2TB SSD

HDD-only alternative: Cheaper but noticeably slower everyday use.

Key concept: Combining storage types for best value
